# OFC 2026 Summary: How Silicon Photonics, CPO, OCI, and OCS Are Redefining AI Infrastructure Interconnect

## Core Thesis

The article argues that AI infrastructure has fundamentally shifted: "In the AI era, the bottleneck is no longer just within the chip itself -- it lies between chips, between racks, and between switches and accelerators."

The central challenge is whether systems can connect at scale with manageable power consumption, latency, and manufacturability.

## Key Industry Shifts

### From Component to System Focus

Optical communications has evolved beyond individual modules and components to become critical infrastructure for AI factories. The industry now addresses a unified question: what should interconnects look like for clusters reaching 100K to 1 million GPUs?

### Scale-Up vs. Scale-Out Architecture

Companies are reconceptualizing interconnect strategy along two dimensions:
- Scale-up requiring ultra-low latency and synchronization efficiency between accelerators
- Scale-out emphasizing throughput, elasticity, and cross-rack scalability

## Technology Progression

### Silicon Photonics Maturation

The 300mm manufacturing transition represents a genuine inflection point, though device-level advances alone prove insufficient. Manufacturing capacity and ecosystem integration present the actual barriers.

### Bandwidth Evolution

The conference emphasized moving beyond 1.6T specifications toward 3.2T and 400G/lane architectures as the actual near-term battlefield.

### Interconnect Boundary Movement

Integration is progressively moving closer to compute:
- From pluggable transceivers to co-packaged optics to optical components integrated within packages

## Critical Validation Milestone

Meta's presentation on 90-million-hour reliability validation for CPO addressed the industry's hardest unsolved problem -- demonstrating that integrated photonics can meet production reliability standards.

## Strategic Implications

The value center in optical interconnects is shifting from individual modules toward integrated platforms and advanced packaging solutions, affecting Taiwan's semiconductor supply chain positioning and the broader industry's competitive dynamics.
