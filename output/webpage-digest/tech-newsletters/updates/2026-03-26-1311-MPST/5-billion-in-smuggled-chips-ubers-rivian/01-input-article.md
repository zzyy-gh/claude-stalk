# $2.5 Billion in Smuggled Chips, Uber's Rivian Partnership, Trustpilot AI Use Surges

## The $2.5 Billion Argument Against Export Controls

US prosecutors charged three individuals connected to Super Micro Computer, including co-founder Yih-Shyan Liaw, with orchestrating a scheme to divert advanced NVIDIA chips to China through Southeast Asian intermediaries using falsified paperwork and duplicated serial numbers. The operation allegedly generated approximately $2.5 billion in revenue during 2024 and 2025, with Liaw potentially facing up to 20 years in prison if convicted.

Super Micro, accounting for 9% of NVIDIA's revenue, stated it was not named as a defendant and placed Liaw on administrative leave. This case highlights the fundamental tension in US chip export policy: balancing short-term leverage against China with long-term consequences. The Trump administration attempted to navigate this by permitting NVIDIA H200 exports in December 2025 in exchange for a 25% US government fee, while continuing to restrict newer Blackwell chips.

The smuggling operation demonstrates that significant demand can motivate market circumvention of restrictions. The primary effect of export controls may ultimately be enriching intermediaries rather than meaningfully slowing Chinese AI advancement.

## Uber Signs $1.25 Billion Deal with Rivian

Uber partnered with Rivian to develop robotaxis based on the R2 SUV, with the arrangement valued up to $1.25 billion, including a $300 million initial investment and 10,000-vehicle order. The fleet deployment targets San Francisco and Miami in 2028, expanding to 25 cities across North America and Europe by 2031 exclusively through Uber's platform.

However, significant risks accompany this partnership. Rivian has not yet initiated R2 production, lacks a deployed robotaxi-ready autonomous driving system, and its Georgia manufacturing facility remains under construction. This represents Rivian's fourth major deal preceding profitability, following $1.3 billion in Amazon equity and vans, a $5.8 billion Volkswagen Group joint venture, and a $6.6 billion Department of Energy loan.

Rivian's existing self-driving feature "will not stop or slow down for traffic lights or stop signs," suggesting substantial development remains before achieving true autonomous capability.

## Trustpilot Surges on LLM Citations

The Danish review platform became the fifth-most cited domain by language models in January 2026, with click-throughs from LLM references increasing 1,490% year-over-year. As chatbots direct more traffic to Trustpilot, businesses increase their presence there, driving organic growth that generates additional chatbot referrals.

Trustpilot's annual profit nearly quadrupled in 2025, propelling stock gains exceeding 30%. The platform succeeded where competitors failed by maintaining data openness. Yelp aggressively restricts scraping and API access, while Google Reviews remains locked within Google's ecosystem. Trustpilot's advantage also stems from serving e-commerce and software businesses rather than primarily local physical establishments like competitors.

AI models favor Trustpilot's timestamped human opinions for recommendation and shopping queries, valuing authentic user perspectives as reliable signals.
