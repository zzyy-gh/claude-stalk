## Articles

### [An Interview with Arm CEO Rene Haas About Selling Chips](https://stratechery.com/2026/an-interview-with-arm-ceo-rene-haas-about-selling-chips/)
**Source**: [Stratechery](https://stratechery.com) | **Published**: 2026-03-26 | **People**: Rene Haas (CEO, Arm), Ben Thompson (Stratechery)

Arm CEO Rene Haas discusses the company's historic shift from pure IP licensing to selling its own chips, marking Arm's first-ever standalone keynote. The move is driven by AI's acceleration of the need for tighter hardware-software integration -- Haas argues that if you own the ISA, the chip is not the product, the system is. Meta is the first customer for Arm's own chips. The interview traces Arm's evolution from accidental mobile success (Nokia GSM, then iPhone) through compute subsystems to now competing directly with Nvidia's CPUs in data centers, with cloud-native workloads and Nvidia's own shift from x86 to Grace (ARM) providing the ecosystem foundation. Haas acknowledges the risk of alienating licensees but frames it as existential: in an AI world where models evolve faster than hardware, platform owners must control more of the stack.

### [Anthropic stopped building a Chatbot and started building an Operating System](https://linas.substack.com/p/fintechpulse1061)
**Source**: [Linas's Newsletter](https://linas.substack.com) | **Published**: 2026-03-26 | **People**: --

Linas argues that Anthropic has pivoted from building a chatbot to constructing an AI operating system. The latest Claude mobile update embeds functional instances of tools like Figma, Canva, and Amplitude directly within the chat interface -- users can prompt, edit, and sync changes back to source applications from their phones. This positions Claude as a "Super App for all knowledge work," comparable to WeChat but for the AI era _(author's characterization -- Anthropic has not used this framing officially)_. The strategic implication is that Anthropic is competing not just on model quality but on platform lock-in through workflow integration.

### [OFC 2026 Summary: How Silicon Photonics, CPO, OCI, and OCS Are Redefining AI Infrastructure Interconnect](https://tspasemiconductor.substack.com/p/ofc-2026-summary-how-silicon-photonics)
**Source**: [Semivision](https://tspasemiconductor.substack.com) | **Published**: 2026-03-26 | **People**: --

Semivision's OFC 2026 conference summary argues that AI infrastructure's bottleneck has shifted from compute to interconnect -- the challenge now lies between chips, racks, and switches. The industry is moving beyond 1.6T specs toward 3.2T and 400G/lane architectures. Silicon photonics is maturing with 300mm manufacturing as a genuine inflection point, and integration is pushing closer to compute (from pluggable transceivers to co-packaged optics to on-package integration). Meta's 90-million-hour CPO reliability validation was a critical milestone, addressing the industry's hardest production-readiness question. The value center is shifting from modules to integrated platforms and advanced packaging, with implications for Taiwan's semiconductor supply chain.
