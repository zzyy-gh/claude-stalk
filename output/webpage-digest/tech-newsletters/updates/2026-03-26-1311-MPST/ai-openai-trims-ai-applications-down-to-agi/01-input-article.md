# AI: OpenAI Trims AI Applications Down to 'AGI' Basics

OpenAI is narrowing its focus by shutting down its Sora video app to concentrate resources on core AI models and enterprise solutions, positioning itself against competitor Anthropic ahead of major IPO races.

## Strategic Shift to Fundamentals

CEO Sam Altman has relinquished oversight of safety and security teams to concentrate on "raising capital, supply chains and building datacenters at unprecedented scale." This represents a deliberate pivot away from consumer-facing applications toward enterprise-focused AI infrastructure.

The discontinuation of Sora--which reportedly cost roughly $5 billion annually while generating under $500,000 monthly in revenue--frees substantial computational resources. Industry estimates show the app had earned approximately $2.1 million in lifetime in-app purchases, making the math unambiguous for pursuing more efficient uses of computing power.

## Organizational Changes

Fidji Simo, formerly of Meta, has been retitled from "CEO of Applications" to head "AGI Deployment," signaling a strategic reorientation toward artificial general intelligence deployment rather than diversified product development.

The company has completed initial development of its next major AI model, codenamed "Spud," expected to be "very strong" within weeks. This advancement could accelerate computational redistribution from Sora's infrastructure.

## Competitive Pressures

These moves reflect OpenAI's competition with Anthropic for enterprise customers and investor attention as both companies approach anticipated blockbuster IPOs within the coming year. Anthropic has been gaining traction with AI agents for coding and enterprise tasks.

## Collateral Impact

Walt Disney abandoned its planned $1 billion investment in OpenAI related to Sora licensing, underscoring the commercial consequences of the shutdown decision.

The Sora research team will now prioritize "world simulation research especially as it pertains to robotics," exploring how AI can learn to simulate environments at high fidelity--a longer-term strategic investment in physical automation capabilities.
